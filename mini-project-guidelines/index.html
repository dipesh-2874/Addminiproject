Contribution Rules

Mini Project Submission Guidelines

Follow these rules before opening an issue or submitting a project. These checks are mandatory to keep participation fair for everyone.

1 Active project at a time
7 Days Gap before next submission
Own Issue Only self-raised issues allowed
Back to How to Contribute Go to Home

Important

Submissions that break hard restrictions can be closed without review. Please verify your issue and timing before starting work.

Core Rules

01

Hard Restriction

One active project at a time

A contributor can submit only one mini project at a time.

02

Hard Restriction

7-day gap between submissions

To submit another project, there must be a minimum 7-day gap after your previous submission.

03

Hard Restriction

Work only on your own raised issue

Contributors can only work on issues raised by themselves. Picking issues raised by others is not allowed.

Submission Flow

  1. Raise a new issue describing your mini project idea.
  2. Wait until your issue is assigned to you.
  3. Build and submit only that assigned project.
  4. After submission, wait at least 7 days before opening a new one.

Why these rules exist

  • Prevent misuse of issue allocation.
  • Maintain fair participation across contributors.
  • Keep the submission process clear and consistent.

FAQ

Can I work on two small projects at the same time?

No. Only one active mini project is allowed per contributor at a time.

When can I submit my next project?

You can submit your next project only after a full 7-day gap from your last submission date.

Can I pick an issue opened by another contributor?

No. You can only work on issues that you raised yourself.
